# IPD & Agile Glossary

**Part of**: IPD Toolkit Transformation Plan Collection

## A

**Agile-Stage-Gate**: A hybrid project management model that retains the
Stage-Gate (TR) macro governance framework while using Agile methods
(Scrum/Kanban) for micro execution within each stage. Combines "discipline"
(gates) with "agility" (iterations).

## C

**CBB (Common Building Block)**: Reusable software components, modules, or
libraries shared across multiple products or features. IPD promotes CBB reuse
to reduce duplication and accelerate development.

## D

**DoD (Definition of Done)**: A checklist of criteria that must be met before
a work item (story, feature, or release) can be considered complete. Includes
code quality, testing, documentation, and security checks.

**Dual-Track Agile**: A development approach with two parallel tracks:
Discovery Track (exploration, validation, prototyping) and Delivery Track
(implementation, testing, deployment). Ensures only validated features enter
development.

## L

**LPDT (Lead Product Development Team Manager)**: The IPD role responsible
for coordinating the PDT, managing cross-team dependencies, and facilitating
Technology Review (TR) gates. Maps to the RTE (Release Train Engineer) in SAFe.

## M

**MoSCoW**: A prioritization technique categorizing requirements as Must-have,
Should-have, Could-have, and Won't-have. Used in the IPD requirement intake
process.

## P

**PDT (Product Development Team)**: A cross-functional team in IPD that
includes all competencies needed to deliver a product: product management,
architecture, development, testing, and operations.

**PO (Product Owner)**: The Agile role responsible for defining user stories,
prioritizing the backlog, and accepting completed work. Maps to the IPD
Product Manager role.

**Product Trio**: A cross-functional discovery team composed of the Product
Owner (PO), System Architect, and UX Designer. Together they validate feature
desirability, feasibility, and viability before development commitment.

## R

**RTE (Release Train Engineer)**: The SAFe role responsible for facilitating
ART (Agile Release Train) events, managing dependencies, and driving continuous
improvement. Maps to the IPD LPDT role.

## S

**SDD (Spec-Driven Development)**: A development methodology where
specifications are treated as executable artifacts that directly generate
working implementations. The foundation of the vibe-ipd toolkit.

## T

**TR (Technology Review)**: IPD gate reviews that assess project readiness
at phase boundaries. The 6 TR gates are:
- **TR1**: Concept/Feasibility Review
- **TR2**: System Architecture Review
- **TR3**: Detailed Design Review
- **TR4/TR4A**: Development Increment / Gray Release Readiness
- **TR5**: System & Beta Test Review
- **TR6**: Launch Readiness Review

## W

**WSJF (Weighted Shortest Job First)**: A prioritization method that divides
the business value (including time criticality and risk reduction) by job
size. Higher WSJF scores indicate higher priority. Used for data-driven
backlog prioritization in SAFe and IPD.
